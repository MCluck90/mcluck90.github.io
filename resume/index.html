About Me

I love programming, exercising, reading, writing, and breakfast. Initially I got into programming, like many people, because I wanted to make video games. I even wrote my own game engine. While this is something I still like to dabble in on the side, my focus has shifted. I specialized in web development and got reasonably good at it. Creating things that people can interact with is what I find most enjoyable about programming and web development is a good venue for creating interactive tools.

In that same vein, I got very involved in programming languages and compilers. A programming language is really just a user interface for the programmer for instructing the computer and I want to make that interface better. Web developer by day, compiler hacker by night. Learning more about computers and people is what I live for.

Recently, I've fallen in love with Rust. It's a fantastic language and, in my opinion, will become a critical tool for many people in the near future.
